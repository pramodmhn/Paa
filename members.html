Pentair is the first accredited Profibus International Competence Centre (PICC) in Australia. The PICC is not affiliated to any vendor and is thus able to provide an independent service for the implementation of Profibus technology. The Centre offers the services of qualified Profibus experts, hardware equipment from a variety of vendors and system test lab operations.

Pentair's automation centre contains a wide variety of control systems and instrumentation for potential Profibus users and suppliers to become acquainted with the technology using their devices of choice in a user-friendly environment. The PICC's capabilities include on-site support during installation and commissioning; instrument configuration and site verification. The PICC also offers certified Profibus Installer and Profibus Engineer training courses, certificate qualifications recognised by Profibus International.

Siemens was one of the founding companies involved in the initial development of Profibus and continues to be a major supporter of Profibus and Profinet International. Schneider Electric Case Study

Built on time in 29 months and around $90 million under budget, Sydney's $1.8 billion desalination project includes a 250 million litre per day capacity plant at Kurnell and an 18-kilometre pipeline. The project was designed to provide a non rainfall dependent drinking water source. This case study provides an overview of the key partners involved in the project and explores Schneider Electric's role in delivering the desalination plant. It identifies best practice approaches that were implemented to ensure the project was delivered on time and under budget.
